reflection

The ideal audio format to add on a website depends on individuals needs. I personally decided to use mp3 format for my audio files because it can be played on most browsers and devices, and maintains a decent balance between audio quality and file size.
But as I mentioned before, one might think about using other formats like AAC, OGG, or WAV if they need to maintain a better level of audio quality. While these formats may produce higher file sizes, they provide audio quality that is better than MP3.

Regarding the usability I tried to consider the following points:

  • I tried to make the audio accessible to all users by providing a transcript. By clicking on the "Transcript" button user can hide or show the transcript.
  • I provided user with control over audio. Meaning user can play/pause the audio, and adjust the volume and playback speed.
  • The audio won't autoplay upon opening the page as it can be annoying for users.
  • I chose mp3 to give the user a decent quality with a small file size so they can open the file even if they have a poor internet connection. Also, mp3 is compatible with a wide range of browsers and devices.
